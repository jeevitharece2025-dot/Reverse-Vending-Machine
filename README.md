# Reverse Vending Machine (RVM) – IoT-Based Automated Recycling System

## Project Overview
The Reverse Vending Machine (RVM) is an IoT-enabled system that automates waste collection, segregation, and recycling. Users can deposit plastic, glass, or metal containers, and the machine verifies, sorts, and rewards them in real-time. Data is sent to the cloud for monitoring, analytics, and optimization. This project is ideal for malls, campuses, smart cities, and public spaces.

---

## Problem Statement
Valuable recyclable waste is often lost because it is mixed, poorly sorted, and lacks consumer incentives. Cities and businesses face high costs in manual waste collection, low recycling rates, and challenges in meeting ESG/CSR compliance.

---

## Solution
The RVM solves this by:
- Automating material recognition and sorting using sensors and microcontrollers.
- Providing instant rewards to users to encourage recycling.
- Sending data to the cloud for analytics, reporting, and operational efficiency.
- Reducing manual labor and operational costs for waste management.

---

## Key Features
- Supports plastic, glass, and metal waste.
- Automated material verification and sorting.
- LCD display for user interaction.
- GSM/Wi-Fi connectivity for cloud data upload.
- Rewards users via coupons or digital points.
- Generates actionable waste collection data for cities and organizations.

## Working Principle
1. User inserts a recyclable container.  
2. Sensors detect type and weight of the material.  
3. Microcontroller verifies material and triggers sorting mechanism.  
4. Reward is issued instantly via LCD or app.  
5. Data uploaded to cloud for analytics and reporting.

# Large-Scale Plastic Management: Recycling & Roads

This project outlines a practical strategy for managing **huge amounts of collected plastic** through **Large-Scale Recycling** and **Plastic Road Construction**. It is suitable for municipalities, startups, and industrial setups.

---

## Large-Scale Recycling

**Goal:** Turn collected plastic into **usable raw materials** for manufacturing.

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Segregation**
   - Separate by type: PET, HDPE, LDPE, PP, etc.
   - Remove contaminants: metal, paper, organic waste.

2. **Shredding**
   - Cut large plastics into smaller flakes using industrial shredders.

3. **Washing & Drying**
   - Remove dirt, labels, and residues.
   - Use water tanks with detergent, then dry using centrifuge or heat dryers.

4. **Extrusion / Pelletizing**
   - Melt the clean plastic flakes.
   - Form into **granules / pellets** for industrial use.

5. **Quality Check**
   - Test melting point, strength, and contamination levels.

6. **Distribution / Sale**
   - Sell pellets to plastic product manufacturers or use in-house.

### Machines Needed
- Shredder / crusher
- Washer + dryer
- Extruder + pelletizer
- Conveyors & storage silos

### Scale Example
- 1 ton/day plant: ₹50–70 lakh (small setup)
- 10+ tons/day plant: industrial-scale production

---

## lastic Roads

**Goal:** Mix collected plastic with bitumen to build **durable, eco-friendly roads**.

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Sorting**
   - Use LDPE / HDPE plastics (bags, wrappers, packaging).
   - Avoid PVC-heavy plastics.

2. **Shredding / Grinding**
   - Cut plastic into 5–10 mm pieces.

3. **Drying**
   - Remove moisture to prevent road defects.

4. **Bitumen Mixing**
   - Heat bitumen to 160–170°C.
   - Add shredded plastic (~6–10% by weight).
   - Mix until fully coated.

5. **Road Laying**
   - Use standard road construction equipment.
   - Plastic-bitumen mix behaves like normal asphalt.

6. **Finishing**
   - Compact with rollers and cure for a few days.

### Impact
- Stronger, more durable roads
- Water- and pothole-resistant
- Reduces plastic waste (1 km road uses ~1 ton plastic)

### Scale Example
- Municipal roads: 10–50 km → 10–50 tons of plastic
- Highways: Hundreds of tons per year

---

## Combined Strategy

- **Split collected plastic:**
  - High-quality, single-type → Recycling → Pellets
  - Low-value, mixed, or thin plastics → Plastic roads

- **Revenue Streams:**
  - Sell plastic pellets to industries
  - Government road projects (fees + incentives)

- **Environmental Impact:**
  - Reduces landfill and microplastic pollution
  - Contributes to sustainability & ESG goals

---

## Notes

- Avoid open burning or dumping
- Ensure proper pollution control during recycling and road construction
- Collaborate with local municipalities and industrial partners for scaling
